Romantic Love

30-Second Film Study

SOURCE // DRIVE_VIDEO // LIVE

Description & Reflection

It explores how romantic love can be felt through small gestures, and how someone’s presence can transform an ordinary space from isolating to comforting.


"I created a 30 second film that explores romantic love through absence and presence within a single, intimate space. The film begins with a still shot of my desk, quiet and empty, while I sit there visibly frustrated and exhausted. The stillness emphasizes loneliness and emotional weight without dialogue or dramatic movement. In the final moments, the same frame transforms. A lover enters indirectly by placing a card and flowers on the desk. The setting does not change, but the feeling does."

Feedback

"The initial draft was noted as being 'slightly too direct.' The critique suggested moving away from a clear narrative arc to allow the viewer more space for interpretation."

Mechanical Cognizance

Electromechanical Soundscape

SOURCE // DRIVE_AUDIO // LIVE

Description & Reflection

A composition built from noises I used to ignore in my daily life: the hum of an elevator, the sound of a printer, and the whine of a Dyson Airstrait.


"This project focused on how I listen to my environment. I started noticing the noise in machines that I previously found distracting or invisible. By isolating these sounds and layering them, I wanted to highlight these sounds."

Feedback

"I tried to incorporate my feedback from the video project here by not necessarily having a direct message. Some critiques I got was how the ambiguity of some noises (such as the hairdryer) can become more purposeful."
